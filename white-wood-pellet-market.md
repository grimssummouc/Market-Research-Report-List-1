<p><p>The white wood pellet market is categorized into two primary types based on hardness: high hardness and low hardness pellets. High hardness pellets are typically made from dense hardwoods, offering greater durability, energy density, and a longer burn time, making them ideal for industrial applications. In contrast, low hardness pellets, often derived from softer woods, are easier to produce and generally less expensive, catering to residential heating needs. The choice between these types depends on the intended use, efficiency requirements, and cost considerations.</p></p>
