AC input stepper motors operate on alternating current, offering high torque at high speeds, making them ideal for industrial applications. Conversely, DC input stepper motors utilize direct current for operation, providing precise control over position and speed, often suited for smaller, low-power applications. Both types serve distinct purposes, catering to varying demands in automation, robotics, and various precision control applications.</p></p>
