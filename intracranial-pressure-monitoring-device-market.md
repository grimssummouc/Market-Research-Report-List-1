<p><p>The intracranial pressure (ICP) monitoring device market is segmented into invasive and noninvasive types. Invasive intracranial pressure monitoring devices involve the placement of sensors within the skull to provide direct measurement of pressure, commonly used in critical care settings. In contrast, noninvasive intracranial pressure monitoring devices utilize external methods, such as optical sensors or ultrasound, to estimate ICP without surgical intervention. Both types play a crucial role in managing conditions like traumatic brain injuries and monitoring patients in intensive care.</p></p>
