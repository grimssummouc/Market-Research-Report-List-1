<p><p>The robotic-assisted hip replacement market encompasses three main types of systems: passive, semi-active, and active. Passive systems provide guidance for surgeons without automated assistance, aiding precision during procedures. Semi-active systems combine surgeon control with robotic features, enhancing adaptability and stability. In contrast, active systems fully automate surgical tasks, offering high levels of precision and consistent outcomes. Each system type aims to improve surgical efficiency, patient recovery times, and overall outcomes in hip replacement procedures.</p></p>
